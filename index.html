Education

M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering - Digital Electronic Systems

Shahid Beheshti University, Tehran, Iran (Fall 2021 - Summer 2024)

  • Thesis Title: Automatic Recognition of Iranian DASTGAHs by Machine Learning Algorithms
  • Supervisor: Prof. Reza Ghaderi
  • Description: This research focuses on the classification of Iranian Traditional Music modes (DASTGAHs) using machine learning algorithms. The study introduces the use of Error-Correcting Output Codes (ECOC) combined with a Multilayer Perceptron (MLP) network for the first time in this domain. The MLP-ECOC model achieved an accuracy of 96.32%, outperforming other models such as CNNs, RBF networks, LSTMs, and RNNs.

B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering - Electronics

Islamic Azad University, Zarghan Branch, Iran (Fall 2017 - Summer 2021)

  • GPA: 3.2/4.0
  • Final Project Title: Analysis of Prediction for Heart Diseases Using Intelligent Algorithms
  • Supervisor: Prof. Soltani Sarvestani
  • Description: The research investigates the application of various intelligent algorithms for heart disease prediction, addressing challenges in accurate diagnosis within healthcare. Using algorithms such as K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN), Support Vector Machines (SVM), Multilayer Perceptron (MLP), and Naive Bayes, the model achieved an accuracy of 92% in predicting heart disease based on medical data.

Publications

M. Ashena, R. Ghaderi, “Classification (Type Identification) Of Iranian Traditional Music with Combination of Simple Networks"
Journal of New Music Research, 2024, Under Review.

Show Details

This research explores the classification of complex datasets using an ECOC-MLP model. The novelty lies in integrating error correction coding with neural networks, efficiently breaking down multi-class problems into binary tasks. The study achieves 96.32% accuracy, outperforming traditional models with reduced computational complexity. This approach demonstrates the ECOC-MLP's efficiency in handling high-dimensional data, offering better robustness and generalization. Applied to the NAVA dataset, it shows the potential of AI in advancing Music Information Retrieval (MIR) in diverse contexts.

M. Ashena, R. Ghaderi, “Dimensionality Reduction and Neural Networks for Music Information Retrieval: A Case Study of Iranian Traditional Music"
In Preparation.

Show Details

This study evaluates dimensionality reduction techniques using the MLP-ECOC model. PCA, LDA, and CNN-based feature extraction were applied to reduce feature dimensions and assess their impact on model performance. The study highlights the balance between reduced input dimensions and classification accuracy.
